Roasted Chicken

Description

Working at Jewish Summer camp, meals subsisted of bread and cheese. So, Shabbat chicken wings were the highlist of the week. Cooking was still a mystery to me, so seeing my friends dump giant containers of mustard and syrup into a pot of raw chicken took me by surprise.

Years later I was running a kitchen at a Jewish Summer Camp. My staff and I had a competition every Shabbat when we made chicken wings; we each made our own batch and then asked campers whose was best

I won most weeks just by dousing the chicken in dijon mustard and maple syrup(yes my wings cost the kitchen probably too much money)

It wasn't until living by myself that I actualy used this on a roasted chicken in an attemp to "be more Jewish"

Ingredients

  • 1 Whole Chicken
  • 3 Yukon/Gold Potatoes
  • 2 Carrots
  • Olive Oil
  • 1 cup Dijon Mustard
  • 1 cup Maple Syrup
  • 2 tbs White Vinigar
  • Sprig Rosemary

Steps

  1. Cube Potatoes
  2. Slice Carrots
  3. Mix Mustard, Syrup and Vinigar in a bowl
  4. Prehead Oven to 450
  5. In a large cassorol dish, add olive oil, place potatoes, rosemary, and carrots then chicken
  6. Douse in Maple Dijon Sauce
  7. Cook for 45min-1Hr
  8. Finish by Broiling for 1 min
